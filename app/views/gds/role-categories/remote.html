Take part in remote opportunities

Through remote volunteering, you can support people without being on-site.

Opportunities you might find

  • Check in and chat
  • Helpline operator
  • Telephone befriender
  • Peer support volunteer

What your activities could include

  • Calling patients on waiting lists or after discharge
  • Checking on patients' emotional health and wellbeing and helping them feel less lonely and isolated
  • Putting patients in touch with relevant support services

Consider these opportunities if you

  • Want to make a difference to people's lives but can't travel to the volunteering sites
  • Enjoy listening to people from diverse backgrounds
  • Want to use your good communication and interpersonal skills
What you will not have to do

You may be in direct contact with patients but you will not engage in any clinical or therapeutic activities.

You will not have to perform any tasks that require any medical expertise or specialist training. Those tasks will remain under the responsibility of trained health and social care professionals.
