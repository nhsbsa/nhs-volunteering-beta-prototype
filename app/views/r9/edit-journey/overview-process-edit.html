Edit an opportunity listing Edit the overview of the application process

This will show on the opportunity listing under what happens after you register with us.

Guidance

You need to include:

  • how and when you'll get in touch with the applicant
  • what happens after
  • a list of the checks they will have to go through
Example for a specific opportunity

We'll get in touch with you when we review your registration. This might take 2 weeks.

After that, we will book in a chat to get to know you a bit more.

Example for a generic opportunity

We'll get in touch with you when we review your registration. This might take 2 weeks.

After that, we will contact you to discuss which roles are available and how you may support us.

We will book in a chat to get to know you a bit more.

Example if you recruit in cohorts or at specific times

We welcome new volunteers at certain times during the year.

We'll get in touch with you when we review the registration list.

We'll then discuss which roles are available and how you can support us.

Examples of checks for all types of opportunities

There will be different types of checks required before you can start volunteering.

You may need to provide proof of identity and address, and verify that you are eligible to volunteer in the UK.

You may need to complete a criminal records check via the Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS). This is free for volunteers.

You may need to provide personal health and vaccination history information, and attend a health check.

We may ask to provide details of your work and volunteering history, including the details of some referees who we can contact in support of your application.
