Volunteering opportunities

There are many ways you can volunteer with us. We'll discuss with you which are available after you apply.

Arts volunteer

We are always looking for people to help patients with arts and crafts activities on the wards, as well as volunteer musicians who would like to perform live music to inpatients.

Breastfeeding peer support volunteer

Provide support to new parents, through offering help with breastfeeding and breast massage techniques.

Mealtime support

Work alongside staff to support patients to eat and drink and improve their overall experience by providing company during mealtimes.

Palliative care befriender

Provide some practical help, social support and companionship to our most vulnerable and isolated patients. Tasks may include help with medication or equipment collection, light shopping, running small errands.

Visitors' meet and greet

You will be the first point of contact at the hospital. You will meet and greet patients, family members and visitors to the hospital and help make their visit as smooth as possible.

Ward Volunteer

Ward volunteers are part of the ward team, providing support to patients and assisting nursing staff with non-clinical duties.

Am I the right person for this?

This role will suit someone friendly, approachable and caring.

You don't need any previous experience, qualifications or training. All you need is to:

  • have good conversational and listening skills
  • be comfortable approaching people to offer help
  • be comfortable approaching people to offer help

To become a volunteer, you may need to:

  • go through a standard or enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) and occupational health checks
  • prove your right to work in the UK
  • provide details of your work history
  • give the details of two referees

What happens after your apply

Once you have submitted your application to volunteer with us, we will get in touch with you to discuss which roles are available and what you are best suited for.

About Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust

At Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, we have a team of brilliant and valued volunteers who give their time to make a difference for patients and medical staff.

We have a dedicated team of staff who supports our volunteers from the start. We provide comprehensive induction and ongoing training. We can ensure you feel welcome and part of the team. We welcome applications from all sections of the community and are keen to diversify our volunteer workforce.
