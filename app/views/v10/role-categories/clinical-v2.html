Support the delivery of clinical care

You will work with staff to deliver many supportive therapies and services that help recover and improve physical and mental health.

Opportunities you might find

  • Breastfeeding and maternity services support
  • Mealtime support volunteer
  • Pharmacy runner
  • Complementary therapies volunteer

What your activities could include

  • Guiding new mothers to breastfeed their babies
  • Providing nutrition and hydration support
  • Delivering medication for patient discharge and restocking medical cupboards
  • Offering patients complementary therapies like massage, aromatherapy, reflexology, Indian head massage, reiki or meditation to improve their wellbeing

Consider these opportunities if you

  • Want to give back to your local community whilst using your skills
  • Enjoy listening to people from diverse backgrounds
  • Have the required clinical training and qualifications (where relevant)
What you will not have to do

You will only engage in activities within your role-specific training and qualifications. You will receive adequate training when you join and will undertake regular sessions throughout the year.

You will perform tasks within safeguarding guidelines. Clinical staff will be at hand with any support you may need.

Pav's story

Age: 16


Volunteers at: a hospital in Sheffield


As an: art activities volunteer

Why I volunteer: I volunteer with the NHS because I want to gain and build skills and abilities to assist vulnerable patients. I would like to work in the NHS one day.

What I enjoy the most is entertaining patients and making them smile. I have learned new skills and abilities through volunteering. I have gained an understanding of the health and social care sector. It helps me to prepare for my training in the NHS.

My typical day: I volunteer every Saturday from 1-4pm.

I entertain patients with colouring, books and games. We have great conversations and I help them feel a bit better while I am with them.

Why you should consider it too: volunteering in the NHS can help you gain and build up your skills and abilities for the future. You'll feel how grateful patients are to you.
