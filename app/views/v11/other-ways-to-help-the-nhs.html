Other ways you can support the NHS

Volunteering benefits everyone; those you help and you as a volunteer. If you can’t currently find anything that suits you, check back regularly as new health-related roles are added daily to NHS Volunteering.

Volunteering is just one way to help the NHS. Here are other ways you can make a difference in your community.

Volunteer outside of healthcare

The National Association for Voluntary and Community Action can help you find local roles with vacancies. Use the map on their website to find the centre nearest you.

Reach Volunteering connects people to skills-based and trustee roles.

GoVo offers flexible and diverse volunteering opportunities.

Take part in research

Health and care research helps professionals answer important questions and improve treatments for all of us.

Be Part of Research is a free service that helps you to find and join studies across the UK. Register your details, choose conditions you’re interested in and you’ll be sent information on studies you may want to join.

If participating isn’t for you, there are lots of other ways to support research.

Have your say

NHS organisations involve the public in planning services and in decision-making.

Opportunities range from online forums and surveys to joining groups that shape services. 

Visit the NHS England website to view opportunities to get involved, or search ‘patient and public voice’ on your local NHS organisation’s website.

Give blood

Giving blood saves lives. The blood you give is a lifeline in an emergency and for people who need long-term treatments. We need 4,300 blood donations every day to meet demand.

For more information about giving blood, visit blood.co.uk

Join the NHS Organ Donor Register

Organ donation can save or transform lives. Most donations come from people who have died, but living donation is also possible.

To find out more and to register as a donor, visit www.organdonation.nhs.uk

Support NHS charities

Over 230 NHS charities provide extra support for NHS staff, patients, and volunteers. They help the NHS to go above and beyond by funding research, improving hospital environments, investing in state-of-the-art technologies and much more.

Visit NHS Charities Together to donate or find your local NHS charity.

Work for the NHS

There are over 350 careers in the NHS, including patient care and behind-the-scenes roles.

Visit the Healthcare Careers website to learn about careers in the NHS, social care and public health. You can take a short quiz to find the NHS career that best suit you.

Visit NHS Jobs website includes information about current vacancies. You can register to receive regular email updates about jobs that interest you.
