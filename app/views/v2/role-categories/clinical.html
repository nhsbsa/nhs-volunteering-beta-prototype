Support the delivery of clinical care

You will work with staff to deliver many supportive therapies and services that help recover and improve physical and mental health.

For some opportunities, you may need specialist training or qualifications as a pre-requisite. In other cases, you might receive in-house training.

Opportunities that may interest you

  • Breastfeeding and maternity services support
  • Clinical support volunteer
  • Pharmacy runner
  • Pets as therapy volunteer
  • Complementary therapies volunteer

What your day could look like

  • Providing nutrition and hydration support
  • Guiding new mothers to breastfeed their babies
  • Visiting patients with an assessed and accredited dog
  • Delivering medication for patient discharge and restocking medical cupboards
  • Offering patients complementary therapies like massage, aromatherapy, reflexology, Indian head massage, reiki or meditation to improve their wellbeing

Consider these opportunities if you

  • Want to give back to your local community whilst using your skills
  • Enjoy listening to people from diverse backgrounds
  • Have the required clinical qualifications and want to expand your skills and experience
What you will not have to do

You will only engage in work within your role-specific training and qualifications. You will receive adequate training when you join and will undertake regular sessions throughout the year.

You will perform tasks within safeguarding guidelines. Clinical staff will be at hand with any support you may need.
