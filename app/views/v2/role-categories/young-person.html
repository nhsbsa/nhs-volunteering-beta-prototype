Volunteer as a young person

You'll help improve patient experience through a range of opportunities for young people aged 16 to 18.

You'll have a chance to give back to your community and learn about healthcare.

Activities you might get involved with

  • Befriending and providing companionship to patients
  • Helping patients at mealtime and offer refreshments
  • Distributing books, magazines and papers among patients
  • Organising and taking part in arts and crafts activities with patients

What you get out of volunteering

  • Being part of a team and being in contact with people
  • Learning new skills and experience
  • Getting an overview into a career within the NHS

Things to consider

  • Make sure that the opportunities you are interested in are open to 16-18 year olds
  • If you are looking for a work experience, check that your local organisation offers any placements through their learning and development programme
  • Think about how you might fit volunteering around your other commitments like studying and social life
What you will not have to do

You may be in direct contact with patients but you will not engage in any clinical or therapeutic work.

You will not have to perform any tasks that require any medical expertise or training. Those tasks will remain under the responsibility of trained health and social care professionals.
