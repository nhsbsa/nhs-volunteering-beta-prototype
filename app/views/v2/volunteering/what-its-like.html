What it is like to be an NHS volunteer

We have interviewed 196 current volunteers across England about their experience of volunteering within the NHS.

What our volunteers said

  • Volunteering helps you as much as those you support

    "Talking to patients has taught me the importance of listening and how fulfilling a human connection is"
    - Ward support volunteer

  • It's my way of giving back to the community after I have used the service

    "I enjoy being in an environment of care. It means my experience of health issues and recovery is useful and less alienating"
    - Arts and crafts volunteer

  • There is a lot of support available

    "My supervisor is amazing and all the staff involved are very supportive and friendly"
    - Clothes bank volunteer

Meet our volunteers

Our volunteers join us from all groups and communities. They contribute in many ways and for different reasons.

No two experiences are the same but they all make a huge difference to patients and NHS staff.

Meet some of our amazing volunteers and learn what they do and why.

Read our volunteers' stories

Things our volunteers want you to know

  • You do not need experience to volunteer with the NHS
  • You will not carry out duties outside of your responsibilities
  • You get support through regular contact with the coordinators, relevant training, newsletters and access to welfare schemes
  • People think it is a sad place but it is full of hope and optimism
  • There are so many opportunities for volunteers from training to career opportunities that it's a worthwhile investment in your time
  • Volunteering is not just for retired or unemployed - it is flexible to fit around your busy life
