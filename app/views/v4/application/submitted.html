What might happen after you get a reply

These are general steps and they may vary across NHS organisations.

Step 1: meeting the team

You may have an informal conversation with the team to learn more about yourself and the support you can provide.

This could be in person, over the phone or via video conference.

Step 2: getting started

To continue your volunteering journey, you may need to provide:

  • some documents for identity checks (ID, bills or bank statements)
  • your emergency contact details
  • your driving license (where relevant)
  • your right to work in the UK
  • two referees

You may need to go through a Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check. This might take a few weeks to come back.

Step 3: receiving the support you need

Once you are ready to start, you will have a welcome induction.

It will be an opportunity for you to meet the staff and other volunteers. You'll also learn about all the policies and the training you'll need to undertake.

Your team will be there to support you throughout your volunteering journey.
