NHS Volunteering Privacy Policy

Privacy policy

This service (“NHS Volunteering”) is run by the NHS Business Services Authority (NHSBSA) on behalf of NHS England. NHS England will be referred to as 'we' and 'us' from now on.

The NHS Volunteering service provides people with information on volunteering opportunities in the health and social care sector.

Volunteering opportunities are listed on NHS Volunteering by:

  • NHS organisations
  • voluntary, community or social enterprise (VCSE) organisations working with the NHS

Volunteering opportunities are listed on NHS Volunteering by:

Acting on behalf of a recruiting organisation

If you are acting for or on behalf of a recruiting organisation then please read the terms and conditions for recruiting organisations. This will explain your and your organisation's obligations. Please also read our cookie policy for recruiters.

Applying to become a volunteer

If you are considering applying to become a volunteer using this service, please read this privacy policy as well as our terms of use for volunteers and our cookie policy for volunteers.

Some recruiting organisations will use this website to enable you to apply to volunteer with them. In these cases, we will collect information from you and share this with the recruiting organisation.

Some recruiting organisations will provide a link to a third party website which you need to follow to apply. This will take you outside of NHS Volunteering.

If you have questions about a volunteering opportunity or an application, you should contact the recruiting organisation.

Data controllers and data processors

As data controller NHS England is responsible for how your personal information is used, kept, and shared. NHSBSA acts as a data processor for NHS England operating as instructed by NHS England.

What information the service collects

In order to run an effective service we need to collect information about prospective volunteers and recruiting organisations.

Prospective volunteers

As a prospective volunteer the personal data we collect from you includes:

  • your name, address, telephone number, age range and email address when you apply for an opportunity
  • information you provide in support of your application
  • information you share about how we can best support you in your volunteering application
  • questions, queries or feedback you leave, including your email address if you contact NHS Volunteering
  • the details of which device and version of web browser you used
  • information on how you use the site using cookies and page tagging techniques

If you complete the optional equality, diversity and inclusion questions when using our service then we may collect your:

  • age
  • ethnicity
  • religion
  • sex
  • sexual orientation
  • gender identity
  • information about any disabilities you may have.

We will collect any other personal details that you might include in your application through NHS Volunteering.

Recruiting organisation users

As a user from a recruiting organisation the personal data we collect from you includes:

  • your name, telephone number and email address when you register as a user of NHS Volunteering on behalf of your organisation
  • questions, queries or feedback you leave, including your email address if you contact NHS Volunteering
  • the details of which device and version of web browser you used
  • information on how you use the site, using cookies and page tagging techniques

Lawful basis for data collection

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) public bodies like NHS England must have a lawful basis to use your personal information.

Application data is provided by prospective volunteers entirely voluntarily and the Lawful Basis is 6.1(a) Consent. By submitting your data within this service you explicitly consent to your data being used for the sole purposes of applying for a volunteering role as advertised within the NHS Volunteering website.

Equality and diversity monitoring data is provided by prospective volunteers entirely voluntarily and the lawful basis is 9.2(a) consent for special category data.

By submitting your data in response to our Equality and diversity monitoring questions you explicitly consent to your anonymised and aggregated data being used to monitor the service. This enables us to make decisions on how to continuously improve the service and make it more accessible for everyone. These responses are optional and not passed onto recruiting organisations.

The Lawful Basis for NHS England to provide and operate the NHS Volunteering website is 6.1(e) Public Task. This is the basis upon which we collect data about individuals using the service on behalf of recruiting organisations.

Contacting our helpdesk

If you contact our helpdesk service via nhsvolunteering@nhsbsa.nhs.uk or any contact form held within this site, you explicitly consent to your data being used to help resolve or follow up with you about your query, and for your anonymised and aggregated data being used to monitor and improve the service.

How we use your data

For all users of the NHS volunteering service we will collect personal data so that we can:

  • personalise your visits to NHS Volunteering and improve the services that we provide
  • provide customer service and support
  • communicate with you on a personal level
  • measure the performance of our service

Lawful basis for data collection

As a prospective volunteer applying for an opportunity through NHS Volunteering your data will also be used to help us:

  • support you to progress with the application process and share this with the recruiting organisation you have applied for an opportunity with
  • understand the characteristics of people using this service (via optionally provided data) so we can better identify ways in which we can improve the service and make it accessible to all.

Recruiting organisation users

As an individual operating NHS Volunteering on behalf of a recruiting organisation your data will also be used to help us:

  • connect you to prospective volunteers for purposes of supporting their recruitment experience

What we do with your data

For all users of the NHS volunteering service we may share your personal information with:

  • NHS England and the Department of Health and Social Care for reporting purposes. This data will be anonymised and aggregated.
  • any other organisation that has a legal right to it

We will not:

  • pass on your details to any third party or other government department for marketing purposes
  • transfer your personal data outside of the UK

Prospective volunteers

As a prospective volunteer applying for an opportunity through NHS Volunteering we may share your information with the recruiting organisations for which you have applied for a volunteer role.

The data shared with these organisations will be identifiable to you to enable the recruiting organisation to assess your application and communicate with you directly.

A recruiting organisation may:

  • process copies of your application locally in their own systems
  • collect and process additional data as part of their registration/application process.

You should read their privacy policy for more details of this.

Recruiting organisation users

As an individual operating NHS Volunteering on behalf of a recruiting organisation, we may share your information with:

  • Prospective volunteers applying for a role you have advertised

How long we keep your data

We will only retain your personal data for as long as it is needed for the purposes set out in this document or for as long as the law requires us to.

For all users of the service we will keep:

  • your personal information for 24 months from the day you submit your registration
  • your personal information for 24 months from the day your organisation notifies us you are no longer responsible for volunteer recruitment on behalf of your organisation
  • an audit log for 24 months to allow NHS Volunteering processes to be independently checked
  • access log data for 24 months

Prospective volunteers

As a prospective volunteer applying for an opportunity within NHS Volunteering, we will also keep:

  • anonymised information associated with your application in line with NHS England's Corporate Records Retention and Disposal Schedule. This information will be used for reporting purposes only.

How we protect your data and keep it secure

We are committed to doing all that we can to keep your data secure. We have systems and processes in place to safeguard your data and keep strict security standards to prevent any unauthorised access to it.

Your rights

We manage the information you provide as required by Data Protection law. You have the right to:

  • know how and why your data will be collected, processed and stored
  • receive a copy of the information we hold about you on request
  • request to change any information about you that is incorrect or omitted
  • to ask us to restrict our use of your personal data (for example, if you think it's inaccurate and needs to be corrected)

For data processing where the legal basis is consent you also have the right to:

  • withdraw your consent
  • ask us to delete your personal data
  • get a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format

Links to other websites

NHS Volunteering contains links to other websites, including those belonging to recruiting organisations.

This privacy policy only covers the NHS Volunteering website. It does not cover other services and websites that it links to, so you should always be aware when you are moving to another site and read the privacy policy on that site.

Contact us

You may want to get in touch to discuss questions about your personal data and how it is used for NHS volunteering.
