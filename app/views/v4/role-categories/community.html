Support your community

You'll support the delivery of healthcare within your closest community.

Opportunities you might find

  • Community first aider
  • Pick up and delivery volunteer
  • Patient transport service volunteer
  • Ambulance service responder
  • Community response volunteer

What your activities could include

  • Responding to non-emergency situations in your immediate community
  • Providing refreshments to ambulance staff
  • Collecting and delivering medication to patients
  • Transporting patients to and from hospitals and clinics
  • Visiting frail or socially isolated patients following a non-emergency call
  • Assisting patients with essential shopping

Consider these opportunities if you

  • Enjoy helping your local communtiy
  • Wish to support the delivery of frontline care
  • Like meeting new people
What might happen during a visit

You may attend a variety of calls where you will be in direct contact with patients.

In extreme situations, you may need to perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) or use an automated external defibrillator (AED).

You will receive adequate training when you join and will undertake regular sessions throughout the year.
