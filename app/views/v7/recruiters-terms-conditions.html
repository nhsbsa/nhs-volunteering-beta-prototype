Use of NHS Volunteering

NHS Volunteering is a service centrally funded by NHS England.

The following organisations based in England can use NHS Volunteering without charge:

  • NHS organisations including NHS trusts, Integrated Care Boards and GP practices
  • voluntary, community or social enterprise (VCSE) partner organisations to the health and social care sector

When reviewing requests, the NHS Volunteering team will consider some of these points:

  • the impact to NHS health and social care services
  • the volunteering and Recruiting Organisation experience
  • the cost to the public
  • removing any potential for financial gain
  • the potential loss of data or reporting capability

This list is not exhaustive.

Listing and managing volunteering opportunities

You agree to use NHS Volunteering to advertise volunteering opportunities that:

  • relate to live volunteering roles that support the provision of health and social care in England
  • are unpaid or do not involve honoraria (not including expenses)
  • are commissioned or funded by NHS England, Department of Health and Social Care (DHSC), or any other body forming part of the NHS in England
  • have not been filled prior to advertisement

You will close any opportunities that become unavailable.

You agree to respond to all applications submitted by prospective volunteers for your roles and to progress them via your organisation’s volunteer recruitment channels in a fair and equitable manner.

You agree not to use NHS Volunteering in any way that is unlawful, illegal or detrimental to the NHS Volunteering service, the organisations using NHS Volunteering, the volunteers, NHSBSA or NHS England.

Publishing appropriate content

You are responsible for the accuracy of any information you publish on NHS Volunteering. You must not publish any material that:

  • is in violation of any law or regulation that is enforceable in the United Kingdom
  • is illegal, inappropriate or offensive - this includes material for internal use as well as for external publication
  • contains viruses or other malicious software or any tools designed to compromise the security of users, websites and/or systems

You must ensure that your use of the service promotes fair, open competition and equal opportunities for all volunteers.

You must not sell access to NHS Volunteers to any other organisation or user.

Linking to NHS Volunteering

You may link to our website, provided you do so in a way that is fair and legal and does not damage our reputation or take advantage of it. You must not make a link in such a way as to suggest any form of association, approval or endorsement on our part. We reserve the right to withdraw linking permission without notice.

External organisations may link to live opportunities on NHS Volunteering. They may advertise them on their own recruitment platforms.

We reserve the right to move or change our website URLs at any time.

Linking from NHS Volunteering

You are responsible for making paper applications available for volunteers who are unable to submit an online application.

Management of user access

You must designate a minimum of one super user for your organisation and other super users to replace the main super user when unavailable.

You are responsible for ensuring that the super users in your organisation:

  • manage and maintain user access within your organisation, including setting up user accounts and access rights
  • check that approved users are employees of your organisation or of a shared service that manages volunteering recruitment on your behalf and promptly delete accounts for any users that leave
  • ensure that user accounts are set up using your organisation’s email domain address or the domain address of a shared service that manages recruitment on your behalf - for security reasons personal email addresses are not permitted
  • review and where necessary delete user accounts on an annual basis
  • adequately train users within your organisation
  • offer support to users within your organisation and escalate to the NHS Volunteering team when needed
  • organise and co-ordinate super user and housekeeping activities within your organisation
  • cascade any messages sent by the NHS Volunteering team

Data protection

As part NHS Volunteering personal data is shared between NHS England and the Recruiting Organisation. This supports the recruitment of new Volunteers into the NHS and partner Recruiting Organisations.

We share personal data with users of this service to help make informed decisions when:

  • progressing prospective volunteer applicants
  • communicating with volunteer applicants
  • considering the needs and preferences of volunteer applicants

This data sharing will support a high-quality volunteer recruitment experience. The personal data shared should form the basis of a volunteer record held locally in line with your own records retention schedule.

Data compliance and responsibilities

When providing and using NHS Volunteering, NHS England and Recruiting Organisations are each responsible for their own compliance with the applicable Data Protection Legislation.

Data sharing only takes place when Recruiting Organisations opt to receive Applicant Data from NHS England which is submitted via NHS Volunteering.

This does not apply to Recruiting Organisations who use the NHS Volunteering website solely to direct applicants to their own volunteer vacancies’ page or application process, or those who use third party application systems.

Roles and responsibilities of the parties

NHS England and the Recruiting Organisation are independent Data Controllers in relation to Personal Data shared via the NHS Volunteering website. Both parties agree to comply with their obligations as a Controller under the Data Protection Legislation and agree not to do anything which will cause the other to breach their Data Protection Legislation obligations.

NHSBSA is a data Processor of NHS England in respect of Personal Data submitted by a Volunteer prior to their application being submitted to the Recruiting Organisation and is not party to the data sharing obligations set out in this section. Reference to “parties” within this section therefore relate to NHS England and the Recruiting Organisation.

Details of the Personal Data shared between the parties and the Agreed Purpose of the data sharing is set out the table entitled “Data Sharing Activities” below. Both parties agree that Personal Data is only to be shared where it is necessary to perform obligations contained in these Terms and Conditions, where it is required in compliance with the Data Protection Legislation, or where it is necessary for the Agreed Purpose. Both parties agree that they will Process the shared Personal Data only in relation to the Agreed Purpose and in accordance with these Terms and Conditions.

Where one party has provided Personal Data to the other party, the recipient of the Personal Data will provide copies of all relevant documents and information relating to its data protection policies and procedures as the other Party may reasonably require. Links to NHS England’s relevant documents are provided above.

The parties are responsible for their own compliance with Articles 13 and 14 of UK GDPR (Transparency) in respect of their Processing activities. Each party will promptly notify the other party if it becomes aware of any Personal Data Breach relating to Personal Data shared for the Agreed Purposes and shall:

(a) do anything which is reasonably necessary to assist the other party in mitigating the effects of the Personal Data Breach;

(b) implement any measures necessary to restore the security of any compromised Personal Data;
