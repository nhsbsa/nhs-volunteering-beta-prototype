Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust Cardiology Ward Support Volunteer

Apply for this opportunity

Summary

Are you looking for a meaningful way to support patients and gain experience in a healthcare setting? Join Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS trust as a Cardiology Ward Support Volunteer and make a difference in the lives of patients receiving heart care.

What You’ll Do

As a Cardiology Ward Support Volunteer, you will:

  • Provide a friendly and welcoming presence for patients, helping to reduce anxiety during their stay.
  • Chat with patients, offering companionship and emotional support.
  • Assist with non-clinical tasks, such as distributing refreshments and helping patients complete menu choices.
  • Support mobility where appropriate, under staff supervision, to encourage light activity.
  • Help with basic administrative tasks to support ward staff.

What You’ll Gain

  • The opportunity to make a real difference in patient wellbeing.
  • Valuable experience in a healthcare environment.
  • A chance to meet new people and be part of a supportive NHS team.
  • Full training and ongoing support from the volunteer services team.

If you’re passionate about helping others and have a few hours to spare each week, we’d love to hear from you!

Am I the right person for this?

This is the right opportunity for you if:

  • You are friendly, approachable, and compassionate individuals
  • You have good communication and listening skills
  • You are reliable and commited to patient care
  • You're reliabile and want to make a commitment to helping others.
  • You're able to work well as part of a team

What happens after you apply for this opportunity

We'll get in touch with you when we review your request. This might take a few weeks.

After that, we will get in touch with you to discuss which roles are available and how you may support us.

We may book in a chat to get to know you a bit more and walk you through the process.

To become a volunteer, you may need to:

  • provide proof of identity and some utility bills
  • go through a standard or enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) and occupational health checks
  • prove your right to work in the UK
  • provide details of your work history
  • give the details of two referees
About Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust

At Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, we have a team of brilliant and valued volunteers who give their time to make a difference for patients and medical staff.

We have a dedicated team of staff who supports our volunteers from the start. We provide comprehensive induction and ongoing training. We can ensure you feel welcome and part of the team. We welcome applications from all sections of the community and are keen to diversify our volunteer workforce.
